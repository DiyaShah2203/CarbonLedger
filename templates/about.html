About CarbonLedger

Understanding carbon emissions, credits, and ESG compliance

What is Carbon Emission?

Carbon emissions refer to the release of carbon dioxide (CO₂) and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, primarily from burning fossil fuels, industrial processes, and human activities. These emissions are the leading cause of global climate change.

Industrial processes contribute 21% of global emissions
Transportation accounts for 16% of CO₂ output
Electricity generation produces 25% of emissions

What are Carbon Credits?

Carbon credits are tradable permits that represent the right to emit one metric ton (1,000 kg) of CO₂ or equivalent greenhouse gases. They create a financial incentive for companies to reduce their emissions.

1 Carbon Credit = 1,000 kg CO₂
Current rate: ₹800 per credit
Credits can be traded on carbon markets

Why ESG Matters?

ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) is a framework for evaluating a company's sustainability and ethical impact. Environmental criteria consider how a company manages its ecological footprint, including carbon emissions.

ESG-compliant companies attract 25% more investors
Regulatory compliance reduces financial risk
Consumers prefer sustainable businesses 3:1
